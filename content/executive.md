## Why This Matters

When Zapier started measuring AI adoption seriously, they didn't just count who was using the approved tools. They measured everything — including what people were doing on their own.

Their adoption trajectory tells the story: **63% → 77% → 97%**. But the journey to 97% wasn't about deploying better tools. It was about surfacing what was already happening in the shadows and bringing it into the light.

The shadow AI data is staggering — and growing:
- Employees are **3X more likely** to use AI than their leaders expect.
- **80%+ of workers use unapproved AI tools at work**, including nearly 90% of security professionals (UpGuard).
- **78% bring their own AI tools** to work (Microsoft/LinkedIn Work Trend Index).
- **46% would continue using unauthorized tools even if explicitly banned.**
- Only **18.5%** of employees are aware of any official company AI policy.
- **57% of employees** hide their AI prompts at work.
- Shadow AI usage has increased **250% yearly** in some industries.
- Companies with high shadow AI face data breaches averaging **$670,000**.

Walmart's "Ask Sam" tool reveals what measurement looks like at scale: **900,000 associates collectively ask over 3 million questions per week.** That data doesn't just track AI usage — it reveals real-time intelligence about store operations, stocking problems, and customer needs that was invisible before.

Microsoft found the same dynamic from the other direction. By comparing Copilot usage data with Viva Glint employee engagement surveys, they discovered that employees who use AI at least weekly are more likely to report thriving and take initiative. The measurement revealed a positive correlation they wouldn't have seen without looking.

Gallup's 2025 data adds another dimension: **23% of employees don't even know** whether their employer has implemented AI. Nearly a quarter of the workforce is in the dark. And only **16%** strongly agree that their organization's AI tools are useful for their work.

You can't manage what you don't measure. And in AI adoption, the most important things to measure are the ones nobody's reporting.

## The Science

**Nudge theory's social proof** principle states that people are heavily influenced by what others are doing. When you measure and share AI adoption rates — "67% of our team used AI this week" — you trigger social proof that accelerates adoption. When you don't measure, there's no social proof. People assume they're the only ones, or that nobody's doing it.

**Lewin's Force Field Analysis** asks you to identify the forces driving change and the forces resisting it. Shadow AI is a driving force — it shows demand. But unmeasured, it's also a resisting force, because it creates governance risk, security vulnerabilities, and inconsistent practices.

Measuring the shadow transforms a threat into an asset. You learn what tools people actually want, what use cases have natural demand, and where to invest.

## What to Do

1. **Run an AI Usage Census.** A simple, anonymous, 10-question survey (see template below). The goal is to learn what your people are actually doing — not to police them. Frame it that way explicitly: *"This is not about compliance. This is about learning what's working so we can support it."*

2. **Track adoption in your engagement surveys.** Zapier added AI questions to their employee engagement surveys. Microsoft correlates Copilot usage with Viva Glint data. Add at least one AI question to whatever survey you already run.

3. **Create an AI usage dashboard.** Even a simple shared spreadsheet that tracks:
   - Which tools are being used (approved and unapproved)
   - What use cases people are finding
   - How frequently they're using AI
   - What's working and what isn't

4. **Share the numbers.** Once you measure, share openly. "Our team's AI usage rate went from 40% to 65% this quarter." Social proof is the best accelerant.

5. **Connect measurement to every rule.** The census data feeds Rule 1 (revealing real emotions), Rule 3 (identifying zombie pilots vs. real experiments), Rule 4 (finding your shadow AI champions), Rule 7 (knowing which tools need governance), and Rule 8 (tracking whether your infrastructure is working).

## Executive Tool: AI Usage Census

**Instructions:** Deploy this anonymous 10-question survey across the organization. Analyze results at the executive level to inform AI strategy.

---

**AI Usage Census — [Company Name]**

*This survey is anonymous. Its purpose is to understand how AI is being used so we can better support our teams. There are no wrong answers.*

**1.** How often do you use AI tools for work?
- Daily
- Several times a week
- Weekly
- A few times a month
- Rarely or never

**2.** Which AI tools do you currently use for work? (Select all that apply)
- ChatGPT / GPT (free or paid)
- Claude
- GitHub Copilot
- Microsoft Copilot
- Gemini
- Company-approved AI tools (specify: ___)
- Other (specify: ___)
- None

**3.** What do you primarily use AI for? (Select top 3)
- Writing and editing
- Brainstorming and ideation
- Research and information gathering
- Data analysis
- Code generation or review
- Meeting summaries and notes
- Customer communication
- Process automation
- Learning new skills
- Other: ___

**4.** Do you use any AI tools that are NOT officially approved by the company?
- Yes
- No
- I'm not sure what's approved

**5.** If you use unapproved tools, why? (Select all that apply)
- The approved tools don't meet my needs
- I don't know what's approved
- The approved tools are hard to access
- I found something better on my own
- N/A — I don't use unapproved tools

**6.** How much time does AI save you in a typical week?
- More than 5 hours
- 2-5 hours
- 1-2 hours
- Less than 1 hour
- It doesn't save me time / I don't use AI

**7.** What prevents you from using AI more? (Select top 2)
- I don't know what's allowed
- I don't know how to use the tools effectively
- The tools don't work well for my tasks
- I'm concerned about data privacy/security
- I'm worried about job security
- I don't have time to learn
- Nothing — I use it as much as I want
- Other: ___

**8.** How would you rate your confidence in using AI tools? (1-5)
- 1 = Not at all confident
- 5 = Very confident

**9.** What AI training or support would be most helpful?
- Hands-on workshops
- Written guides and tutorials
- Peer mentoring from AI-savvy colleagues
- Dedicated time to experiment
- Examples of how others use AI for my type of work
- Other: ___

**10.** Is there anything else you'd like to share about AI at work?
